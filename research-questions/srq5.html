SRQ5 — What features define an efficient in-store ordering system?

The features that define an efficient in-store ordering system should consists of the following:


  1. User-friendly and intuitive interface
    A friendly and fairly understandable interface engages an automatic swift response from users, knowing that it is an intuitive system. This could stem from aesthetic purposes, but mostly are determined from layout placements based on respect to the heuristic principles, as well as common practices found from other competitors.


  2. Fast and accurate order processing to avoid inefficiency due to errors
    Having a reliable ordering system means that data that is sent must be accurately shown and displayed to users to avoid errors. Having to configure the sizes of certain elements, and placements combined does determine the relevancy of data that is shown; a reference to Hick's Law.


  3. Streamlining processes via integrations with other POS systems
    Allowing third-party integrations enables faster productivity and openness of the platform to connect with other parties, not as a competition but as an opportunity to excel in creating modular and cooperative systems.


  4. Room for personalization and customization
    Having personalization may introduce a sense of 'home' or familiarity for users, and gaining some control or freedom within the domain, with respect to Jakob Nielsen's Heuristic Principles.


  5. Multiple ordering channels
    Accepting an order from a customer is one thing, but accepting orders from multiple customers at once may prove to be a challenge, but results in a faster rate of production and income. The only importance is to have a feasible and large enough space to store these informations without losing priority one after the other.


  6. Gamification & User-oriented programs
    Having a sense of fun user feedback is a rare occurence within ordering applications, as it only serves as a singular purpose of ordering food/beverages. By utilizing gamification ideas, chances to engage with customers could create a lasting impression and serving a space to insert potential upselling ideas, so that customers may want to order more after their first order.


  7. Reporting and analytics
    As mentioned in the earlier research question, having a space that acts as a summary of data is essential to identify trends and set out realistic goals for any organizations. Aided with the right data visualization and ease of data movability (via export), analytics can be a powerful tool for serving real-time data, increasing productivity and reducing action time for for decision-making.


  8. Integrations with other services (delivery/pickup)
    Opening opportunities to other types of services outside in-store ordering requires more handling and infrastructure to manege, but will certainly increase outreach to customers outside of the restaurant. However, since this relates to customers outside of the restaurant, it is not relevant for Obero's current scope.


By incorporating these features, in-store systems become efficient, enhance the customer experience, and streamline order operations and accuracy, ultimately enhancing productivity.



The Approach: How?

Through the field study research done with the owner of Remix, our main client and product tester, the results of the competitive analysis and literature study is realized and grounded upon real case scenarios, with the survey aiding in the discovery of consumers’ desires, and questions regarding the true motivation behind dining experience. This knowledge helps in supporting the aspect of identifying the feature list and defining the aspects of efficiency within a system, eventually being brought up at multiple iterative sessions.
